## Scientific Background for the Fermi Paradox Project

One of the most powerful ways to explore the Fermi Paradox is through galaxy-scale simulation. A dynamic model allows us to estimate how civilizations might spread, communicate, evolve, or go extinct across millions of star systems. Even a simplified simulation can provide insight into why the galaxy appears silent despite the probability of abundant life.

This document outlines the conceptual basis for integrating a galaxy simulation module into the Fermi Paradox Project.

---

## 1. Purpose of a Galaxy Simulation

The goal is not to create a visually complex astrophysical simulator.  
Instead, the simulation should provide:

- Approximate **star positions and density distributions**  
- Models for **habitable zones**, **civilizational emergence**, and **expansion dynamics**  
- A framework for future **machine learning-based prediction**  
- Realistic timelines grounded in astrophysics (stellar lifecycles, travel limits, etc.)

This creates a foundation for evaluating galactic-scale behavior relevant to the Fermi Paradox.

## 3. Civilizational Expansion Models

Different theoretical models can be simulated:

### **3.1 Diffusion Model**

Civilizations expand outward randomly like gas molecules.

### **3.2 Frontier Expansion**

A directed expansion front moves along the simplest reachable paths.

### **3.3 Cluster Growth**

Civilizations colonize in bursts, forming clusters of inhabited systems.

### **3.4 Reversible Expansion**

Civilizations expand but also collapse, resulting in ‚Äúpatchy‚Äù colonization.

Each model explores different Fermi Paradox implications.
